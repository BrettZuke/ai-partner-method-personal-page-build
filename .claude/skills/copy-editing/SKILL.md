# Copy Editing: 7-Pass System

Good copy editing isn't about rewriting — it's about enhancing. Work through 7 focused sweeps, each fixing one dimension. After each pass, verify previous edits remain intact.

---

## The 7 Passes

### Pass 1: Clarity
**Goal:** Every sentence lands on first read.

Check:
- Would a stranger understand this immediately?
- Are there double negatives, nested clauses, or ambiguous "it/they/this"?
- Is the main idea buried in the middle or end of the sentence?

Fix: Move the key idea to the front. Break anything over 25 words.

---

### Pass 2: Voice & Tone
**Goal:** Consistent personality throughout.

Check:
- Does the formality level stay constant?
- Are there sections that sound like a different person wrote them?
- Is the brand voice defined and present?

Fix: Pick a voice. Apply it to every sentence. Cut sections that fight it.

---

### Pass 3: So What (Benefits)
**Goal:** Every feature is connected to a reader benefit.

Check:
- For every claim, can you answer "so what does that mean for me?"
- Are there feature dumps with no "which means..."?

Fix: Add "which means you can..." after every feature statement. Then cut the feature if the benefit alone is stronger.

---

### Pass 4: Prove It (Evidence)
**Goal:** No claim without support.

Check:
- Are there adjectives without proof? ("Best", "fastest", "most powerful")
- Are there statistics, testimonials, or demos that could back this up?
- Is social proof placed near every major claim?

Fix: Replace adjectives with facts. Add specific numbers wherever possible.

---

### Pass 5: Specificity
**Goal:** Replace vague language with concrete details.

Check:
- Any "some", "many", "often", "usually"?
- Any "save time", "make money", "grow your business"?
- Are time frames, numbers, and outcomes specific?

Fix: "Save hours" → "Save 3 hours every week". "Grow your business" → "Add £2,000/month within 90 days".

---

### Pass 6: Heightened Emotion
**Goal:** The reader feels something.

Check:
- Is there a moment where the reader's situation is named exactly as they'd describe it?
- Is the dream outcome painted specifically?
- Does the copy connect to identity, not just outcomes?

Fix: Add one sentence per section that names how the reader *feels* right now. Add one that names how they'll feel after.

---

### Pass 7: Zero Risk (Near CTAs)
**Goal:** Remove every friction point around the call to action.

Check:
- Is there a guarantee near the CTA?
- Are the next steps crystal clear?
- Are there unresolved objections in the reader's head at the moment they're asked to act?

Fix: Add guarantee language. Add "Here's exactly what happens next:" before the CTA. Pre-answer the top 2 objections in the sentence above the button.

---

## After All 7 Passes: Final Check

- [ ] Headline: brand + benefit + news (Ogilvy rule)
- [ ] First 50 words earn the next 50
- [ ] No filler openers ("Here's the thing...", "It turns out...")
- [ ] CTAs use action + benefit language
- [ ] No adverbs
- [ ] Social proof is specific and attributed
- [ ] Mobile reads cleanly (short paras, no walls of text)
