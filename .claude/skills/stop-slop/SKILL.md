# Stop Slop: AI Writing Pattern Elimination

Remove predictable AI writing patterns and produce more human-like prose.

## The 8 Rules

1. **Eliminate filler** — Remove throat-clearing phrases and adverbs
2. **Break formulaic patterns** — Avoid binary contrasts and rhetorical structures
3. **Activate sentences** — Human subjects performing actions, always
4. **Choose specificity** — Replace vague claims with concrete details
5. **Ground the reader** — Address them directly, don't observe from a distance
6. **Diversify rhythm** — Vary sentence and paragraph lengths
7. **Respect intelligence** — State facts plainly without softening
8. **Skip quotability** — Rewrite anything that sounds like a pull-quote

## Patterns to Kill Immediately

### Throat-clearing openers
- "Here's the thing,"
- "It turns out,"
- "The truth is,"
- "What's interesting is,"
- "At the end of the day,"

### False agency
- Inanimate objects performing human actions
- "The complaint becomes a fix" → "We fix complaints"
- "The system allows users to..." → "You can..."

### Binary contrasts (telegraph reversals)
- "Not X, but Y" constructions
- "This isn't about X — it's about Y"

### Passive hiding the actor
- "It was decided that..." → who decided?
- "Users are shown..." → show them

### Adverb abuse
- "-ly" words: "really", "truly", "deeply", "fundamentally"
- Softeners: "just", "simply", "basically", "essentially"
- Amplifiers without proof: "incredibly", "extremely"

### AI-sounding hedges
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "It's important to remember..."
- "One might argue..."
- "There are a number of ways..."

### The em dash overuse
- Every other sentence — like this — signals AI writing

## Scoring Rubric (35/50 = needs revision)

| Dimension | What to look for |
|---|---|
| Directness | No filler, opens with the point |
| Rhythm | Varied sentence length, no monotony |
| Trust | Claims backed by specifics |
| Authenticity | Sounds like a person, not a template |
| Density | Every word earns its place |

## Quick Test

Read it out loud. If it sounds like someone presenting at a TED Talk they didn't mean to give, rewrite it.

If every sentence is the same length, rewrite it.

If you could cut 20% and lose nothing, cut it.
