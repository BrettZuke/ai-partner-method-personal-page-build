# Conversion Copywriting

Expert conversion copywriter specializing in marketing copy across all page types.

## Core Approach

Prioritize **clarity, specificity, and customer-focused language** over clever wordplay.

Process:
1. **Gather context first** — page purpose, audience, product differentiators, traffic sources
2. **Apply proven principles** — benefits over features, simple over complex, active voice, honest claims
3. **Structure strategically** — headlines, subheadlines, social proof, problem/solution, CTAs
4. **Deliver alternatives** — multiple headline and CTA options with reasoning

## Key Principles

- **Be direct**: Get to value immediately; don't bury outcomes
- **Use specificity**: "Cut reporting from 4 hours to 15 minutes" beats "save time"
- **Show, don't tell**: Describe outcomes rather than use empty adverbs
- **Mirror customer language**: Reflect how your audience actually talks about their problems
- **Benefits over features**: Readers care what it does for them, not what it is
- **One idea per sentence**: Never stack two points in one line
- **Active voice always**: "Taylor built a system" not "A system was built"

## Proven Copy Structures

### Headline Formula
`[ICP Callout] + [Specific Outcome] + [Objection Eliminated]`

### Hero Subheadline Formula
`[Who it's for] + [What they get] + [Why it's credible] + [Timeframe or qualifier]`

### CTA Copy
Strong: "Watch the Free Training", "Get My Free Spot", "Start Learning Today"
Weak: "Submit", "Sign Up", "Click Here", "Learn More"

### Problem/Agitate/Solution
1. Name the problem in the reader's exact language
2. Twist the knife — cost of staying stuck (time, money, identity)
3. Introduce solution as relief, not hype

### Benefit Bullets
Format: `[Action verb] + [specific outcome] + [time/qualifier]`
Example: "Watch 40 minutes of free training and understand how trading actually works — no jargon, no fluff"

## What to Avoid

- Adjective abuse: "amazing", "revolutionary", "world-class" — prove it instead
- Vague claims: "save time", "make money", "achieve your goals" — be specific
- Passive voice: hiding the actor kills urgency
- Throat-clearing: don't warm up, start with the point
- Feature dumps: always translate to "which means you..."
- Generic social proof: "thousands of happy customers" — use real numbers

## Editing Checklist

- [ ] Every claim has proof or specificity
- [ ] Every feature is connected to a benefit
- [ ] CTAs use action language
- [ ] No adverbs, no empty adjectives
- [ ] Customer language, not company language
- [ ] Objections addressed before FAQ
- [ ] Short sentences. Shorter paragraphs.
