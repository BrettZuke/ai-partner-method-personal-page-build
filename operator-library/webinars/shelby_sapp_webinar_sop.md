# Shelby Sapp — Webinar SOP

Reference: Shelby Sapp (@shelby.sapp), 80K followers, #1 female sales trainer, largest sales academy for women.
Offer: "She Sells Remote" high-ticket sales training. Price shown on slides: **$5,997**.
Model: Book-a-call webinar → high-ticket coaching/certification program.

Extracted from her full slide storyboard. Use this as a structural reference when building webinars.

---

## What Makes This Different From a Standard Webinar

1. **"Deal?" slide comes before origin story** — she gets explicit audience buy-in before she earns authority
2. **Pre-handles objections before telling her story** — "Maybe you've thought..." comes first
3. **"The AH-HA Moment" is a dedicated section** — not woven into the story, named explicitly
4. **Mystery → Reveal sequence on the offer** — "UNLESS..." hook before the offer appears
5. **"The Real Game Changer" slide** — a dedicated clarity slide that proves the program fixes the exact roadblock she built
6. **Who this is for / not for comes AFTER the offer reveal** — opposite of most webinars
7. **Price shown on slides** ($5,997) — no ambiguity, no "find out on the call"
8. **Teaching the actual sales call structure** — Beginning / Middle / End of call, real tactical depth
9. **Identity framing for the buyer** — "You are getting paid to close lives" elevates their self-concept
10. **Re-introduction at offer time** — restates full credibility when presenting the offer, not just at the start

## Full Slide Structure (In Order)

### FRAME (Slides 1–4)
1. **Title** — "Sales Her Way" with Shelby's photo
2. **Welcome** — sets the room tone
3. **My Main Goal: FRAME** — explicitly frames the webinar and what she wants to accomplish. Not motivational — structural. She tells them exactly what she's going to do and why she's there.
4. **Deal?** — explicit agreement slide. Gets the audience to commit to paying attention and engaging. Creates a psychological contract before she's earned authority.

### PRE-HANDLE OBJECTIONS (Slide 5)
5. **Maybe you've thought...** — lists every objection the audience is already holding. Addresses them head-on BEFORE the origin story. Forces the audience to lower their guard before she asks for their trust.

## Key Structural Principles from This Webinar

### The UNLESS Hook
Before the offer, she tells the audience they are locked from achieving the result **UNLESS** they have the one thing she's about to show them. This creates:
- Urgency (the problem is real and blocking them right now)
- Curiosity (what is the thing?)
- Receptiveness (they're ready to hear the solution)

### The Real Game Changer Slide
A dedicated slide that explicitly connects the roadblock (established in teaching) to the solution (the program). Does not let the audience draw this connection themselves — makes it explicit. Pattern: "You now understand that [roadblock] is what's stopping you. [Program] is the direct fix."

### Teaching the Full Sales Call
She spends 4+ slides on just the End of the Call. This is disproportionate coverage — and intentional. The close is where money is made, so she over-delivers there to prove she has the knowledge and to make the close feel less abstract.

### "You Are Getting Paid to Close Lives"
Identity reframe that makes the skill feel like a calling, not a job. Applied to Dan's webinar: the parallel would be "You are giving creators the tools to build a real business. The revenue share is just how it works."

### Price on Slides
$5,997 shown clearly. No mystery on the call. This pre-qualifies by filtering out people who won't pay, and pre-qualifies by making serious buyers feel confident — they know what they're committing to.

## Shelby vs Standard Webinar SOP — Key Differences

| Element | Standard SOP | Shelby's Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Objection handling | In teaching content | Before origin story |
| Who this is for/not for | Before close | After offer reveal |
| Price | Not shown | Shown on slides ($5,997) |
| Re-introduction | Once, at start | Twice — start + offer |
| Offer framing | As a product | As the fix to a named roadblock |
| AH-HA Moment | Part of story | Its own dedicated section |
| Deal/buy-in | Not standard | Explicit "Deal?" slide early |
