## Design System (applies to every slide)

- **Background:** Pure white `#ffffff`
- **Header bar:** Full-width thick dark charcoal bar at top `#2d2d2d`
- **Footer bar:** Same dark charcoal bar at bottom
- **Typography:** Very heavy/black-weight condensed sans-serif — Impact-style. Massive size for single statements.
- **Text colour:** Pure black `#000000`
- **Alignment:** Left-aligned with left padding
- **Green** `#22c55e` — positive results, key numbers, good outcomes
- **Red** `#ef4444` — bad path, old way, wrong approach
- **No gradients. No icons. No eyebrows. No decorative rules.**
- **Pacing:** 88 slides in ~60 minutes = 1 slide per ~40 seconds

## Key Structural Insights

### 1. Proof before story
Standard webinars: story → teach → prove → sell.
Scott Kelly: **credibility/proof → story → more proof → teach → prove again → sell.**
Audience believes it works before he even explains how he got there.

### 2. One thought per slide
No slide holds two ideas. The presenter talks — the slide shows one statement reinforcing exactly what he just said. Fast advance cadence: ~40 seconds per slide.

### 3. Cascading close
The scarcity section (slides 79–88) is a rapid sequence of short statements, each one adding a layer:
transparency → reason for scarcity → number → guarantee → guarantee details → escalating guarantee → risk reversal → CTA → form.
Each slide is one move. Never combined.

### 4. Teaching is genuinely useful
The 30 slides of teaching are real content — niche tables, outreach math, deal structure tables, funnel diagrams, AI copywriting prompts. Not teasers. This builds credibility and makes the offer feel like a deeper version of what they've already received for free.

### 5. Real screenshots throughout
CRM screenshots, calendar tools, creator search interfaces, real video thumbnails, early Scott videos with embarrassing thumbnails — nothing is mocked up. Authenticity drives trust.

### 6. Green/red colour system
Every list item is coded:
- Green ✅ = good, do this, positive outcome
- Red ❌ = bad, avoid, old way
Used in comparison tables, qualification slides, objection slides. Consistent throughout.

### 7. Testimonials placed twice
First block (slides 32–35) = brief early proof before teaching.
Second block (slides 69–76) = long named proof AFTER the offer bridge.
The second block directly follows the "2 paths" slide — you've heard the offer, now here's 8 people who already took it.

## How to Apply This to Dan's Deck

1. **Restructure the section order** — move proof/stats earlier. Don't save all credibility for after the story.
2. **Break every multi-bullet slide into individual statement slides** — if a list has 4 items, consider 4 slides
3. **Add real screenshots** — Dan's HighLevel dashboard, creator revenue reports, calendar showing client slots
4. **Run a scarcity sequence at the close** — cascade individual statements one per slide (limited cohort → reason → guarantee → guarantee terms → risk reversal → CTA)
5. **Add a testimonial block after the offer bridge**, not just at the end
6. **Use the green/red comparison table** for Old Way vs AIO Way — row by row
7. **The teaching section should be genuinely useful** — actual niche lists, outreach math, revenue model breakdown. Don't tease — teach.
8. **Embed the application form or direct link visually** — make the final slide the CTA form, not a vague "apply now" text
