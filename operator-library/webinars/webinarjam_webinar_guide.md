# WebinarJam — Webinar Strategy Blueprint

Source: Kartra/WebinarJam guidebook. Framework for planning, creating, and promoting a live video event.

---

## Key Benchmarks (Use These for Planning)

| Metric | Benchmark |
|---|---|
| Registrant show rate | 40–50% |
| Attendee conversion rate | 20–40% |
| Optimal webinar length | 45 minutes |
| Q&A benefit rate | 92% of attendees benefit |
| Video on landing page | Boosts conversions up to 80% |
| Day-of registrants | ~25% register on the day |

**Registration target formula:**
- Goal: 10 sales at 30% conversion → need 33 attendees
- 33 attendees at 45% show rate → need **73 registrants**
- Formula: `(sales goal ÷ conversion rate × 100) ÷ show rate × 100 = registrants needed`

## Phase 1: Planning

### Funnel Objectives
Define the webinar's role in the funnel before building anything:
- Generate new leads
- Nurture existing leads
- Engage existing customers to improve retention

Set measurable targets: number of sales, attendees, and registrants.

### Target Persona
Outline the attendee's:
- Demographics and profession
- Interests and location/time zone
- Preferred communication channel
- Awareness level (cold, warm, hot)

Use persona to determine: topic angle, format, length, and promotion timing.

**Examples:**
- Professional seeking business solutions → email at 9:30am on weekdays, not Instagram at 6pm
- Personal skill learner → evening/weekend event, optimized for mobile, social media promo
- Parents → fast-paced, actionable early, provide on-demand recording for catch-up

### Topic
- Most attendees give 45–50 minutes before dropping off
- Go narrow and deep — don't cover everything, cover one thing well
- Topic should communicate exactly what people get if they attend
- Avoid industry jargon, share information people can't find with a simple search

Key questions:
- What problem is my target persona trying to solve?
- How can I help them solve it?
- What steps do they need to take?
- What are the key takeaways?
- What do I want them to do after the webinar?

### Formats
- **Expert Presenter** — single host + slides. Best for shorter webinars, focused topics.
- **Expert Interview / Fireside Chat** — guest expert. Generates interest + exposure in their network.
- **Product Demo** — best when seeing the product in action is essential to selling. Always end with Q&A + CTA.
- **Panel Discussion** — multiple experts. High value, exposure across all panelists' networks.

### Event Details Checklist
- [ ] Webinar title
- [ ] Brief topic description
- [ ] Guest speaker list (if applicable)
- [ ] Date
- [ ] Time
- [ ] Cost (free or paid)

## Phase 2: Creating

### Content Outline Structure
Every webinar should cover:
1. Introduction
2. Overview of the webinar
3. Core presentation
4. Closing
5. Q&A time

**Length:** 45 minutes is proven. Err on brevity — never pad for the sake of length.

### Interactive Elements to Plan
- **Q&A Sessions / Live Chat** — highest value engagement feature. 92% of attendees benefit. Can be moderated or open. May need a second person to manage chat.
- **Polls and Quizzes** — "Who here is doing X?" / "What's your biggest challenge?" Keeps attention. Provides insight.
- **Videos or Demos** — pre-record sections that can run on autopilot. Inject video clips for examples.
- **Bonus Resources / Giveaways** — downloadable templates, exclusive videos, landing pages with special offers. Incentivize attendance.

### Map CTAs
Plan where every CTA fires in the presentation. Should appear:
- During teaching (soft CTAs, conditioning the click behavior)
- At the offer reveal
- During social proof
- At the close
- In Q&A

## Phase 3: Promoting

### Registration Landing Page — 9 Essential Elements
1. Action-oriented headline
2. Event date and time
3. Countdown timer (creates urgency — especially for the 25% who register day-of)
4. Cost / Free label
5. Sign-up form (first name, last name, email)
6. Description of topic with bullet-point benefits
7. Speaker bio
8. Testimonials
9. Instructions for watching

**Optional but high-impact:** Compelling video — boosts landing page conversions up to 80%.

**Scarcity tactic:** Limit spots if format suits a small group. Shows real constraint.

**A/B test:** Headline, messaging, CTAs, element position (top vs bottom).

### Registration Email Series (Pre-Event)
4 automated emails from registration to event start:

| Email | Timing | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Confirmation | Immediately on registration | Thank you, what to expect, add to calendar |
| Reminder 1 | 24 hours before | Tease exclusive content, success stories, short video |
| Reminder 2 | 1 hour before | Direct link, what they'll walk away with |
| Reminder 3 | At start time | We're live — join now |

If using SMS, send Reminder 2 via text as well.

**Enrich reminder emails with:** short clips from past events, testimonials from past attendees, exclusive tips to whet appetite.

### Outreach & Promotion Plan
Match promotion channel and timing to persona:
- Reach professionals → weekday emails 9am–3pm, LinkedIn
- Reach lifestyle/personal interest learners → evenings and weekends, social media, podcasts
- Always promote at the time they're thinking about the problem, not when they're in a different mindset

Channels to consider:
- Email list (if open rate is consistent)
- Social media (match platform to persona)
- Industry publications or newsletters
- Network opportunities (partners, affiliates, collaborators)

### Post-Event Follow-Up Series
4-part email sequence for all registrants (attendees and no-shows):

| Email | Timing | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Follow-up 1 | 1 hour after event | Thank for attending, recording coming soon |
| Follow-up 2 | Within 24 hours | Recording link + slide deck + materials. Soft CTA recap at end. |
| Follow-up 3 | 48 hours after | Recording link + full focus on next-step offer. CTA first, expand benefits. Use urgency/expiry if applicable. |
| Follow-up 4 | 3–4 days after | Social proof (testimonials), additional resources, final offer reminder with urgency |

**Rule:** If a user converts at any point, remove them from the sequence immediately.

### Evergreen Automation
Once a live webinar is high-converting, convert it to an automated on-demand webinar:
- Set up evergreen registration page
- Set up evergreen registration emails
- Set up evergreen follow-up emails
- Let it run while planning next live event
