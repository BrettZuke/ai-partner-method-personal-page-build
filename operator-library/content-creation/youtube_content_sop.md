# YouTube Content SOP

**Purpose:** Build trust, shift beliefs, and convert high-quality leads through long-form video content.

---

## Content Funnel Flow

```
Instagram → YouTube → VSL → Appointment Set → Booked Call → Closed Sale
```

---

## Why YouTube?

- **Higher Quality Leads** — Long-form content attracts more serious, engaged prospects
- **Trust at Scale** — Nothing builds authority and connection like someone watching you speak for 20+ minutes straight
- **Effortless 7-Hour Rule** — Studies show buyers typically consume ~7 hours of content before making a purchase. YouTube lets you hit that threshold faster than any other platform
- **You Can't Fake Long-Form** — It's harder to BS in a 20-minute video. This makes you more real, more likable, and more trustworthy

Long-form content that builds deep trust, positions you as an expert, and reframes the way your audience thinks is insanely valuable — they actively want to work with you.

## YouTube Content Workflow

### 1. Find a Repeatable Process

Build a system your creator can stick to consistently. Even 1–2 videos per week will compound over time. A creator pulling in only a few thousand views per video could easily pull in $10k+/month.

---

### 2. Finding Video Ideas (Most Important Part)

The idea determines everything. No edit, no camera, no hook will save a bad idea.

**Where great ideas come from:**
- Common objections from your sales calls
- Questions your clients are asking
- Content that genuinely inspires you
- Content you disagree with (strong opinions = attention)
- Your journey: mistakes, lessons, failures, beliefs
- Viral content in your niche (reverse-engineer the format or angle)

Niche down aggressively on your video topics. Treat YouTube like a search engine — craft your titles and positioning as if your ideal lead is actively searching for a solution to that exact problem.

---

### 3. Validating the Idea

Once you've got a potential idea, answer these four prompts:

1. What do you want them to **know**?
2. Why do you want them to know it?
3. What do you want them to **do**?
4. Why do you want them to do it?

If you can't clearly answer all four, you don't have a validated idea yet.

Also: **Create your thumbnail and title at this stage.** These are your hooks. Without a good hook, no one clicks.

---

### 4. Prepping the Video

Don't script word-for-word. Break your video into 4 main sections with bullet points under each. Practice riffing off those points.

The more you film, the more natural and passionate you'll become. You want to sound real, not robotic. Remember — people are buying from YOU. They buy from who they know, like, and trust.

---

## Video Structure: 4-Part Framework

### Part 1 — What Is It?
- Restate the title/topic. Validate why they clicked.
- Promise them what they'll learn and why it's worth watching.

### Part 2 — Why Is It Important?
- Why should they care?
- What will this do for their life/business?
- Why should they trust you? (State your proof or experience.)

### Part 3 — How Does It Work?
- Teach it in your way: 1 method, 3 steps, 5 pillars — whatever fits
- Use personal stories, analogies, and examples
- This builds connection and credibility

### Part 4 — What Should They Do Now? (Strong CTA)

Choose **one** primary CTA:
- Watch another video (great for subs and binge time)
- Book a call / Apply via Typeform
- DM on IG/WhatsApp

## YouTube Videos That Print

**Client Interviews**
Showcase real people who got real results. These hit trust and relatability hard.

**Case Study Breakdowns**
Teach your audience how you helped a client succeed. Show the process.

**Belief-Shifting Rants**
Destroy outdated methods. Introduce your new vehicle/solution.
Example: *"Stop dropshipping like it's 2018. Do this instead."*

---

## Key Metrics to Track

**Click-Through Rate (CTR)**
- Target: 5–10%
- Measures how many people clicked vs. how many saw it
- Influenced by: Thumbnail, Title, Topic
- Use VidIQ for insights

**Average Watch Time**
- The longer people watch, the better the video performs
- Insight into: Energy, idea strength, pacing, retention

## Content Creation Rules

- **Don't copy your niche — disrupt it.** If everyone's doing polished tutorials, be raw, loud, opinionated, or stripped-down.
- **Avoid generic "how-to" content.** It's boring. It makes you look like another guru.
- **Be raw, not robotic.** If you can film it in one take with minimal editing, do it. People follow people they connect with — not personas.
- **You do not need flashy edits.** A Miro/Loom video of you going over a specific topic is all you need. Just put captions on.
- **Don't fake it.** Talk like you would to a friend. Be honest. Be imperfect. People trust real.
