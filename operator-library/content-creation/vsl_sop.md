# Crafting a VSL

A VSL needs to include **ethos & pathos**. Logos is for the sales call.

**Key Principles:**
- Do not sell the "thing" in your VSL
- Build a strong connection with the creator — they need to trust the creator as the messenger and feel like they're being spoken to 1:1
- Speak in a highly convicted tone, be genuinely passionate
- Sell why the creator is the person to take them from A to B — not how. Frame the sales call or group as the how

**The VSL Must Ensure Leads Score 10/10 On:**
- Do they trust the creator?
- Do they trust in the way/mechanism?

## Goal of the VSL

Nurture prospects to buy your program by educating them and getting them to like you BEFORE they hop on a sales call. Everyone must watch this video before they get on a call.

> **WARNING:** Never sell the process of the product in the VSL. You must sell the idea of them working with you.

- How can you portray their story so they bond with you over their insecurity/fears?
- How can you make it so they want to work with you and only you?

70% of the VSL should be talking about the creator and their story. The VSL must handle ALL objections a lead may have. Human nature is bonding over insecurities and hardships — the VSL builds that connection.

## VSL Structure

### 1. Hook

The most important part. It determines how many people watch to the end. If you do the hook right, your retention will skyrocket.

**Hook Formula (pick one):**

**Callout:**
> "If you're interested in achieving [desired outcome] without [common pain point #1, #2, and #3], I made this for you."

**Creator's Success:**
> "Here's how I achieved [desired outcome] without [common pain points #1, #2, & #3]."

**Free Value:**
> "This is how anyone can achieve [desired outcome] without [common pain points #1, #2, & #3]."

---

### 2. Transformation

**Goal:** Make your story so relatable they think: *"Damn, this person was exactly like me."*

Sections:

1. **Intro** — What made you start? What was the moment? Tie this to the viewer's current situation.
2. **Failure** — You tried everything and kept failing. This was rock bottom.
3. **Pain** — Describe what you were going through. Use pain points of your ideal client. Get emotional.
4. **Solution** — Then came the big realization that changed everything. The "aha moment."
5. **Transformation** — That realization took you from rock bottom to the top. Paint the picture:
   - How you felt
   - What it meant to you
   - How others looked at you
6. **New Program** — Now you're launching a program to help others achieve [desired goal] in [time period] — but it's not for everyone.

---

### 3. Case Studies + Testimonials

Once they believe in your transformation, show them proof you've done it for others. Use your best client results.

For each client go through:
- Their story
- Current situation
- Problem
- How you helped
- Desired outcome

They'll resonate with your past clients and think: *"If it worked for them, it can work for me."*

**Goal:** Get them hooked and hungry to know how you did it.

---

### 4. Call Out

**Goal:** Make sure your ideal clients feel like it's made for them. If someone shouts "Hey," you turn around. If they shout your name, you're locked in.

**Who This Is For:**
- List 5–10 bullets
- Describe how they're feeling and their current situation
- E.g., "This is for people who are action takers and implement fast."

**Who This Is NOT For:**
- List who you don't want in the program
- Boost their ego — make them think "Yeah, that's not me. I'm different."
- E.g., "This isn't for people who think they can get rich overnight."

---

### 5. Blaming Their Failures

- Explain why everything they've tried hasn't worked
- Relate this to your story
- Trash your competitors: "They've been doing it wrong the whole time."
- Rekindle their belief that it can work

---

### 6. Why Your Offer Is Different

- Categorize your product as one-of-one — they can't compare it to competitors on price or value
- Make your offer irresistible
- Emphasize what no one else is offering
- Make them feel like they'd be foolish not to join

---

### 7. The Biggest Lie

Call out the false belief holding them back.

Example:
> "You don't need to scale by yourself or invest thousands into a coach that just tells you what to do."

- **Unsustainable:** You'll burn out sending 100 outbounds a day and taking 10+ sales calls
- **Instead:** Work with a Done-For-You team that's scaled coaches to $50k–$100k/month
- **Strategy:** You just do a bit of marketing and fulfillment. We handle everything else — messages, sales calls, sales ops

---

### 8. The 4-Step Process

Now that you've:
- Described their situation better than they can
- Blamed their past failures
- Proved why your offer works

Present your 4-step process:
- Go specific in detail
- Make it different from competitors
- Use cool names for each step

---

### 9. Next Steps

At this point they should be sold. Tell them what to do next:

- Fill out a Typeform (pre-qualifies budget)
- DM a keyword on IG/WhatsApp
- Book a call
