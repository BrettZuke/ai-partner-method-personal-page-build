# VSL Landing Page Design & Copy System

Sourced from: Swipe Pages, Demand Curve, Primer, KlientBoost, Ryan Zo Fay, Unbounce

---

## The Conversion Equation (Master Principle)

**Conversion Rate = (Desire + Confidence) / (Effort + Confusion)**

Every design and copy decision either increases desire/confidence or reduces effort/confusion.
Nothing else matters.

---

## Page Anatomy — Exact Section Order

1. **Top bar** — Urgency strip (countdown, seat count, or event date)
2. **Hero** — Headline + sub + social proof number + CTA above fold
3. **Logo bar** — Media/credibility logos (even small ones count)
4. **Problem statement** — Name the exact pain, use audience's language
5. **Framework/process** — Numbered steps showing HOW it works (removes fear)
6. **Social proof #1** — Specific results with numbers ("4,300 members", "93/100 score")
7. **Benefits + objections** — Side-by-side "For you / Not for you" grid
8. **Social proof #2** — Testimonials with specifics, not vibes
9. **Instructor bio** — Authority stacked: credentials + platform numbers + photo
10. **Value stack + price** — Assign values to bonuses, anchor high, reveal real price
11. **Guarantee** — Specific terms, visible, framed as investor protection
12. **FAQ** — Address the 3-5 objections that kill conversions (not collapsed)
13. **Final CTA** — High contrast, dark or gold bg, benefit-led button copy
14. **Footer** — Disclaimer, legal, socials

## Hero Section Formula (Above the Fold)

Must include **at least 5 of these 7 elements**:

1. **Scannable headline** — One sentence, benefit-first, under 12 words
2. **Subheadline qualifier** — Who it's for + what they'll get + credibility nod
3. **Social proof in hero** — Star ratings, member count, or a one-line testimonial
4. **CTA button** — Above fold, contrasting color, action language
5. **Deal/offer visible** — Free, discounted, or limited — say it immediately
6. **Product/founder image** — Real photo, not stock. Lets user connect to the person
7. **Eye-flow direction** — Arrow, gaze direction, or shape pointing toward CTA

### Headline Formula

**[ICP Callout] + [Specific Transformation] + [Objection Killed]**

Example: "The Skill Getting Men Out of the 9-5 — Without a Business Idea, a Degree, or Money You Can't Afford to Lose"

Or use: **[OFFER] + [BIG PROMISE] + [SPARKLE]**
Sparkle = the hook/angle that makes it different from every other promise they've seen

Supporting tips:
- Use numbers/statistics where possible
- Superlatives work (first, only, fastest)
- Active voice always
- One benefit, one idea — never stack benefits in headline
- Subheadline does the qualifying work, not the headline

## VSL Video Placement Rules

- Video is the HERO — center it, give it space
- Surround with: thumbnail credibility + play button + one-line hook above it
- Always gate: email opt-in before play unlocks (captures lead even if they don't watch fully)
- Use Wistia for turnstile email gates
- Never autoplay — kills trust
- Add subtle "Watch now →" copy under the play button

## Trust Signal Strategy — Stacking Order

Stack trust signals in layers, not all at once:

**Layer 1 (Hero):** Big number — members, students, followers, sales
**Layer 2 (Below hero):** Media logos / credibility sources
**Layer 3 (Mid page):** Specific testimonials with results and names
**Layer 4 (Bio section):** Platform authority — YouTube subs, Instagram followers, podcast stats
**Layer 5 (Near CTA):** Guarantee + risk removal

Rules:
- Specific > vague ("4,309 TWT members" not "thousands of students")
- Visual proof > text proof (screenshot, dashboard, certificate image)
- Disqualify bad fits explicitly ("This is NOT for...")
- Answer "who else believed you?" before they ask it

---

## CTA Rules

- Repeat 5-6 times throughout the page (video is long, decision happens at different moments)
- Change supporting copy between CTAs — not the same button every time
- Button copy: commitment language ("YES, I'M READY") or benefit ("Watch the Free Training")
- High contrast — gold on black, white on dark, never grey on grey
- Never say "Submit" or "Click Here"
- Exit intent popup with separate offer or softer ask

## Copy Frameworks

### PAS (for headlines + problem sections)
**Problem → Agitate → Solution**
- Name the problem using their exact language
- Twist the knife — make them feel the cost of staying stuck
- Introduce the solution as relief, not hype

### AIDA (for VSL script + page flow)
**Attention → Interest → Desire → Action**
- Hook in the first 5 seconds
- Build interest with story/proof
- Create desire with transformation + social proof
- Drive action with clear CTA + urgency

### Disqualification (for "For you / Not for you" sections)
Explicitly state who it's NOT for. This increases trust with the right buyer.
- "Not for you if you want overnight riches"
- "Not for you if you won't put in 1-2 hours/week"
- This filters out bad fits AND makes ideal buyers feel more selected

### Objection Handling in Copy
Address objections in-flow (not just FAQ):
- "You might be thinking..." → preempt then neutralize
- "I know what you're thinking — this sounds like every other trading course"
- Layer objection kills into the framework section and bio section

---

## Urgency & Scarcity Rules

- Real deadlines only — vague pressure doesn't convert
- Countdown timer at top bar AND bottom CTA (two placements)
- Seat/spot limitations with real numbers ("23/50 spots taken")
- Price expiry — show when the price goes up and by how much
- Date-specific language ("This closes Friday at midnight")

## Visual Design System

### Color Strategy
- **Dark background + gold/white accent** = premium, trust, authority
- **Red CTAs** = urgency (use sparingly — works for event/deadline scenarios)
- Limit to 3 colors: background, accent, CTA
- High contrast always — accessibility = conversion

### Typography
- Headlines: Bold geometric sans (Syne, Neue Haas Grotesk, Monument Extended)
- Body: Clean readable sans (Inter, DM Sans)
- Use type scale aggressively — massive H1, smaller H2/H3
- White space = luxury signal. Don't cram.

### Layout Principles
- Alternating light/dark sections to break monotony and signal new chapters
- Full-width sections for social proof and CTAs
- 2-column for "For/Not for" and testimonials
- Mobile-first: 70% of traffic is mobile — design hero for 390px wide first
- Sharp corners (3-4px radius) = credibility. Overly rounded = soft/cheap.
- 3D product mockups where possible — adds perceived value

### Visual Hierarchy
- One focal point per section
- Directional cues (arrows, eye gaze toward CTA)
- Icon grids for feature/benefit lists — scannable
- Stats call-outs: large number, small label below

---

## Guarantee Section

- Specific terms, not "money-back guaranteed"
- "If you watch the full training and don't find one actionable strategy, email us and we'll refund immediately"
- Frame as: you're so confident in their success, you're removing all the risk
- Place once mid-page + once near final CTA

---

## Value Stack / Pricing Section

- List everything included with assigned values
- Cross out the total value, reveal real price below
- Show what they'd pay elsewhere for equivalent ("£500/hr coach, £200 course, £150 community = £850 value")
- Payment plan option if price is high

---

## FAQ Rules

- 5-7 questions max
- Don't collapse — expanded by default
- Lead with the killer objection ("Is this a scam / is this legit?")
- Answer honestly, including who it's not right for
- Close FAQ section with a CTA

---

## Mobile Optimization Rules

- Hero CTA button full-width on mobile
- Video 16:9 with large play button (min 80px tap target)
- Testimonial cards: full-width stack, not side-by-side
- Nav: sticky or hidden — never a burger menu that buries the CTA
- Font minimum: 16px body, 32px+ headline
- Load time under 2 seconds — no heavy background videos

---

## What Separates Top 1% VSL Pages

1. They lead with authority + specific numbers in the first 3 seconds
2. They break the transformation into a visible numbered framework
3. They use disqualification to pre-filter buyers
4. They repeat CTAs with evolving context (not copy-paste)
5. They assign specific monetary values to everything in the offer
6. They use real urgency — not fake countdown timers
7. They answer objections inside the main flow, not just in FAQ
8. Their testimonials include specifics: name, number, before/after
