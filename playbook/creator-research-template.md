# Creator Research Dossier — Template

> Fill this out **before** reaching out to any creator you want to partner with. The pitch lands when you understand their audience, their offer, their bottleneck, and their dream state. Cold pitches that skip this step get ignored.

**How to use:** copy this file, rename it `<creator-handle>-research.md`, and fill in every section. Use a mix of: their public content (last 30 days), their bio + pinned, their audience comments (read 200+ across their top videos/posts), their existing offers (free + paid), and what's said about them in third-party content (podcasts they've been on, articles, Reddit threads).

Time to complete one dossier: 60–120 minutes for a creator you don't know. 30 minutes if they're in a niche you already understand deeply.

---

Build a single table with every platform they're on. Note follower count, average engagement (where visible), and posting frequency. **Don't skip platforms with low followers** — sometimes the smaller channel is where the real audience lives.

| X / Twitter | @ | | | |
| Other | | | | |

Add: peak record (best performing single post/stream), bio as of today, any clear current focus (launch, tour, new offer, etc.).

## 2. Who They Are

3–5 sentences. Real name, age, location, origin story (how they grew, what got them their first 10k followers), what they're known for. Get this from their About page, podcast appearances, or interview content.

**Key facts** (bullets):
- Co-founders / business partners — who?
- Notable press, features, or media moments
- Brand symbols / running jokes / catchphrases the audience knows
- Side ventures or owned brands

## 3. What Their Content Is Actually About

Don't just say "fitness" or "trading" or "lifestyle." Be specific about the **modes** of content and the **patterns** that win.

### Per platform — top 5–10 posts/videos

| Asset | Platform | Engagement | Core theme |
|---|---|---|---|
| | | | |

### What patterns win
After tabling 5–10 top-performing posts, name the pattern. Is it transformation stories? Provocative takes? Behind-the-scenes? Vulnerability? Tactical breakdowns? Drama? Numbers/proof? Be specific. The pattern tells you what the audience actually shows up for.

### Comment language & tone
Read 200+ comments. Note the **slang, vocabulary, in-jokes, and emotional tone**. Are they pet-naming each other? Using community-coded language? What words appear over and over? This is the language you'll write opener DMs in.

## 4. Who Their Audience Is

### Demographics
- **Gender split:** ~%
- **Age range:** core ___ to ___, peak ___
- **Location:** primarily ___, secondary ___
- **Class / income proxy:** student / working class / middle / professional / high-income
- **Cultural identity / scene:** what subculture do they belong to?
- **Cross-platform behaviour:** where else do they spend time?
- **Adjacent creators:** who else does this audience watch / follow / buy from?

### Psychographic profile
What do these people **actually want, fear, struggle with, and aspire to**? Write 5–10 bullets in their voice — first-person, raw, no euphemisms. Examples:
- "I want to make ___ but I don't know where to start."
- "I'm scared that ___ will never change for me."
- "I admire people who ___."

This is where most operators stop too early. Push for specifics until you can write a DM that sounds like one of them, not like an outsider.

## 5. Pain Points

What's broken in their audience's life that this creator addresses? List 5–10. Then group: which are surface-level (visible in content) vs. underlying (the real driver)? The pitch will hit the underlying ones.

## 6. Dream State

If everything goes right for this audience in 12 months, what does their life look like? Specific outcomes — money, relationships, status, freedom, body, identity. Use the language they'd use, not generic transformation copy.

## 7. What They Already Sell

Audit every offer, free and paid. Build a table.

| Offer | Type | Price | Where it sits in funnel |
|---|---|---|---|
| | (lead magnet / product / coaching / mastermind) | | (TOF / MOF / BOF) |

Note: do they have a **clear ladder** or just scattered offers? Is there a **missing middle** between their lead magnet and their high-ticket offer?

## 8. Monetisation Gap Analysis

Compare what they sell to what their audience actually wants.

- **Best-performing content theme** → is there an offer that monetises it directly? (Often: no.)
- **Comments that ask "where can I learn more?"** → do they have a product that answers that?
- **Implicit promises in their content** → are those promises kept by the offer ladder or only by the content?

This section identifies the **leak**. The pitch is about plugging it.

## 9. The Core Insight

In one paragraph: what's the **one thing** about this creator's business that's leaving money on the table? Examples:
- "Their audience asks how to get fit but their only offer is supplements — there's no education product."
- "They have 200k followers and one $19 PDF — no $500 / $2k / $10k tier."
- "Their content is converting strangers to fans, but they have no setter or system to convert fans to buyers."

The core insight is what the partnership is *about*.

## 10. Recommended Offer / System Architecture

Based on the gap, propose:
- **What you'd build first** (the highest-leverage AI system from the AI Partner Method stack — DM Setter, Content Systems, Funnel Infrastructure, Sales Operations, Offer Architecture, or Analytics)
- **Why it's the highest-leverage move** (in 2 sentences — tied directly to the gap from §9)
- **Estimated impact** (rough — "+30% on call volume", "+£10k/month from existing traffic", "convert their 80k IG audience to a 5k email list")

If the partnership runs longer, sketch the **full ascension funnel** they'd grow into:
- Free / discovery
- Front-end product (£0–£100)
- Core offer (£500–£3k)
- High-ticket / mastermind (£3k+)

## 11. Key Messaging Rules for Their Audience

Critical for writing the DM, the email sequence, the sales page, the ad copy. List 6–10 rules. Examples:
- ✅ Use working-class British slang ("mate", "proper", "sorted")
- ❌ Don't use guru language ("transform", "manifest", "abundance")
- ✅ Lead with outcome before mechanism
- ❌ Don't reference your other clients in the pitch — they care about themselves, not your roster
- ✅ Match their content's tonal energy — punchy if it's punchy, calm if it's calm

These rules become the **voice constraints** when you write copy for them later.

## 12. Risks and Watch-Outs

What could blow up the partnership?
- **Reputation risk** — controversies, content that conflicts with brands, audience that turns
- **Operational risk** — they post infrequently, they have a co-founder dispute, they're already exiting
- **Offer risk** — their existing offer doesn't actually deliver; you don't want to amplify a bad product
- **Personal fit** — their content style doesn't match your strengths

Be honest. If too many risks light up, walk.

## 13. One-Page Summary Card

When you're ready to pitch, this is what you reference. Compress everything above into a single readable card.

```

```

---

## Output: the pitch

You don't write the pitch from a template. You write it from this dossier. The pitch is **one paragraph** that hits:
1. The leak you noticed (§8)
2. Why it matters (§9)
3. What you'd build (§10)
4. The proof that you understand their audience (§4 + §11 — show, don't tell)
5. The ask (one specific next step — "got 15 minutes for me to show you what I'd build?")

If your pitch could be sent to any creator, you didn't do the dossier work. Send it back. Re-research.
