# Copywriting — Synthesis Checklist
*Source: Copy Elite — Copywriting 101, Synthesis module (final module)*

This is the master checklist. Run every piece of copy through all 4 phases before it goes live.

---

## Phase 1 — Foundations
*Before you write anything, answer these.*

**1. Who are you writing to?**
- What are their specific conditions? (beliefs, situation, desires, fears)
- See: Specific Conditions framework

**2. What is the micro-action goal of this piece?**
- What do you want them to DO right now?
- Examples: click the CTA, open the next email, watch the video, reply

**3. What is the macro-action goal of the system this piece lives in?**
- What is the bigger outcome this piece is contributing to?
- Examples: build trust, create desire for the offer, move them to a purchase decision

**4. What are the rules of the environment?**
- Every platform has norms. What are they?
- Examples: email subject line length, IG caption format, YouTube title conventions, cold DM tone

---

## Phase 2 — Rough Drafts
*Get it out. Don't edit yet.*

**1. What is the one "big idea" of this piece?**
- Summarize the entire sales message in one sentence
- Is it simple, useful, and compelling?

**2. What is the main emotion you're pushing?**
- Pick from the 4 General Conditions: New, Easy, Safe, Big
- Is this the most compelling emotion for this audience right now?

**3. What sections do you need, and in what order?**
- Map the structure before you write

**4. Write as much volume as you can without judgment**
- Just write. Don't think, feel.
- Try to capture the raw emotion you're going for

**5. Write as many rough drafts as you need until the emotion comes through**
- The more important the copy, the more drafts you should write

---

## Phase 3 — Refined Draft
*Now make it better.*

**1. How can you make the emotions MORE potent?**
- Focus on benefits, not features
- How does the reader FEEL about their current situation?
- Can you paint a more vivid, specific, painful picture?

**2. How can you create more trust?**
- Apply the 11 Variables of Likeness
- See: Liking-Relatability framework

**3. Does the voice match the brand?**
- Does the language sound like the person/brand writing it?
- Are you using words that resonate with this specific audience?
- Make it authentic — not generic

**4. How can you make the hook/angle more curious?**
- What question are you implying in their head?
- Would a different question create more curiosity?
- Can you tie more emotion to it?

**5. Have you added urgency and/or scarcity?**
- Is there a reason to act NOW?
- See: Urgency-Scarcity framework

---

## Phase 4 — Final Draft
*Cut, clarify, and tighten.*

**1. Remove and fix anything confusing**
- Would someone who knows nothing about this understand it?
- See: Concise-Curiosity framework

**2. Remove and fix anything unbelievable**
- Does every bold claim have proof, logic, or specificity behind it?
- See: Concise-Curiosity framework

**3. Remove and fix anything boring**
- Is every section earning its place?
- See: Concise-Curiosity framework

**4. Simplify and condense**
- Cut every word that isn't pulling weight
- Shorter is almost always better

**5. Wait 24 hours, then read it again**
- Fresh eyes catch everything

**6. Repeat Phase 4 as many times as the importance of the piece demands**
- A launch email gets 3+ passes. A quick story post gets 1.

---

## Quick Reference: The Full Loop

Foundations → Rough Draft → Refine Emotions/Voice/Curiosity/Urgency → Final CUB Pass → Simplify → Rest → Ship
