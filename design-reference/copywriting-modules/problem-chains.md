# Copywriting — Problem Chains (Value Creation & Unique Mechanisms)
*Source: Copy Elite — Copywriting 101, Problem Chains module*

---

## The Value Creation Chain

**Pain → Problem → Solution → Effectiveness = Value Created ($)**

Each step must happen in order:
1. **Pain** — If they're not in pain, they won't look for a problem to solve
2. **Problem** — If they don't connect their pain to a problem you solve, they'll never find your solution
3. **Solution** — If they don't believe your solution is the best/only one for that problem, they won't choose you
4. **Decision** — If they don't believe they need to act NOW, they'll procrastinate (find another problem to blame, wait until pain increases, or get forced by urgency/scarcity)
5. **Effectiveness** — Value created = how well your solution actually solves the problem (or how well they THINK it does)

---

## Value Is Relative

Value is created in the mind and depends on which core desire the person is currently trying to fulfil (Maslow hierarchy).

Key insights:
- Someone solving bottom-tier needs (survival, safety) doesn't care about status or self-actualization
- Someone solving middle-tier needs (status, belonging) is locked in "chasing the bag" — they don't care about enlightenment yet
- Someone who already has money and time cares about purpose, legacy, higher-tier desires
- **Your product/solution is only valuable to people stuck on the level your solution actually helps with.** Selling Level 3 cheat codes to someone who hasn't beaten Level 1 = zero perceived value
- Selling to people with money is powerful because each dollar is worth less to them AND they're focused on higher-tier desires that are harder to solve (meaning they'll pay more)

**Breakthrough:** What you sell doesn't have to be "worth a million bucks" — it just has to be worth MORE to the buyer than the money they paid for it.

## Pain Buckets (Problems)

Problems are "pain buckets" — containers people blame their pain on.

**Create Pain → Attach to Problem → Desire to Solve Problem**

The more pain you associate with a specific problem, the more pressure they feel to solve it. Your goal: attach as MUCH pain as possible onto the specific problem your product solves.

**Critical insight:** The exact same pain points can be blamed on completely different problems. Your product isn't just competing against your niche's competitors — it's competing against every other possible solution to those same pain points.

You MUST show them why the problem you solve is the TRUE source of their pain.

## Unique Mechanisms

A unique mechanism is a **specific, intriguing name** for the specific problem your solution solves.

Effective unique mechanisms create: curiosity/intrigue, New/Only emotion, Big/FOMO emotion.

**A good unique mechanism must be:**
- **Specific** — not vague
- **Unique** — not something they've heard before
- **Causal** — logically connects to their problem
- **Simple** — easy to understand

When used incorrectly, unique mechanisms come across as salesy and inauthentic. They're 100% dependent on specific conditions — what sounds "unique" to one audience could sound boring or overused to another. Audiences evolve and become more aware over time.

**Warning:** It doesn't have to be some crazy scientific thing. It's just a logical angle that makes your solution sound unique and exciting.

---

## Sub-Mechanisms

Sub-mechanisms are unique mechanisms WITHIN your main mechanism that solve the micro-problems involved in achieving the desired result. They're designed to destroy limiting beliefs about your opportunity.

**How to create effective sub-mechanisms:**
1. Break down what your mechanism achieves into a simple 3-5 step process
2. Make sure each step targets the biggest false beliefs they have about succeeding
3. Name each step using the same rules as unique mechanisms (Specific, Unique, Causal, Simple)

Sub-mechanisms aren't necessary in every scenario. Treat them as powerful tools in your toolbox — not always needed, but great to have.

## The Problem Chain

We solve big problems by breaking them into smaller problems. Subconsciously, people form "problem chains" — mental to-do lists of what they think will fulfil their core desires.

**If you can prove that your unique mechanism is the final domino that leads to them solving their bigger problems, they'll have an overwhelming urge to buy.**

You do this by mapping out the problem chain that logically leads your audience to the conclusion that they NEED your product/service.

### The Problem Chain Structure (Top → Bottom)

1. **Core Desire(s)** — The internal problem they're trying to solve, the real origin of their pain (usually unaware of this)
2. **The Big Problem / Goal** — The obvious external problem they currently blame their pain on. Their current awareness level. (e.g. "Make money," "Lose weight")
3. **Problem Fractals** — The problem "to-do list" they believe will lead them to achieving the big goal. Increasingly specific sub-problems.
4. **Unique Mechanism** — The unique problem/opportunity that YOUR product/service solves
5. **Sub-Mechanisms** — The unique solutions to the micro-problems involved in your big mechanism

### Building the Chain — Key Questions
1. What are the core desires your audience is trying to fulfil?
2. What is the obvious external problem they currently blame their pain on?
3. What is the unique mechanism / specific problem your product solves? (Specific, Unique, Causal, Simple)
4. What is the logical chain of increasingly specific problems from the obvious external problem → to needing your unique mechanism?
5. What sub-mechanisms fall under your unique mechanism?

### Rules
- Keep it simple — what makes the most logical sense to reach the conclusion?
- There is NO "perfect" problem chain. There are many angles/paths to sell something. Find a path that is compelling to YOUR specific conditions.
- Don't overthink it. You'll discover/refine the best problem chain over time.
- It takes practice and iteration. Worth every hour you spend on it.
