# Copywriting — Specific Conditions
*Source: Copy Elite — Copywriting 101, First Principles module*

---

## The Core Idea

Copywriting = words written to change beliefs and cause action.

The formula: **Reader + Words = Action** (or lack thereof)

Your copy is a key. The reader's mind is the lock. You can't craft the right key without first understanding the lock.

Three truths that follow from this:
- You can't write great copy without understanding who you're writing to
- The better you understand the reader, the easier and faster you write great copy
- Copy that works for one audience can completely fail for another

---

## Two Types of Conditions in Every Reader's Mind

### 1. General Conditions (All Humans)
Pre-programmed thinking patterns — the monkey brain. These aren't taught, they come pre-installed.

Key example: **brains want to conserve energy, so they prefer the path of least resistance.**

Practical application: make your offer sound EASY. Make your copy EASY to read. No matter how smart someone is, when being sold to, they want it simple.

Other general conditions: New, Easy, Safe, Big, Fast, Free — these appeal to almost all humans.

### 2. Specific Conditions (Niche-Specific)
The psychological, physical, and situational conditions shared by the people you're targeting.

These are: their past experiences, current situation, desired future, pain points, language they use, perceived problems, beliefs about why they can't succeed.

**This is where the real power is.** Finding ONE extremely potent specific condition can drive millions in sales from the emotion it generates over time.

---

## The Two Keys to Leveraging Specific Conditions

### Condition Scalability
Your audience isn't 10,000 carbon copies of the same person. Your job is to find which specific conditions are shared by the **largest portion** of your audience.

Don't target a condition felt by 20% — target the condition felt by 80%.

### Condition Potency
Don't find a million conditions. Find the **few that matter most** to your audience and focus your messaging there.

Example: your audience might want to lose weight for 5 different reasons. Research reveals the one they want most is to be more attractive. That's what you build your copy around.

**Quality > Quantity. Sniper > Shotgun.**

---

## The Three Categories of Specific Conditions

**Macro Aim** — audience (who), purpose (why), goal (what)

**Painful Details** — current situation, dream situation, pain points, desire points

**Beliefs** — method false beliefs, internal false beliefs, external false beliefs, perceived micro-problems

---

## How to Find Specific Conditions

**Method 1: Sales/Discovery Calls** *(most powerful)*
Get on calls with people in the audience — or get recordings from your client. Dig until you hit real emotion. The transcripts are gold.

**Method 2: Personal Experience**
Writing for an audience you've been part of = you already know more than you think. Use your own experience to fill out the conditions first.

**Method 3: Search-Based Research**
- YouTube: find popular channels your audience watches → check most popular videos → read comments (questions, positive/negative reactions)
- Reddit: find the subreddit → most popular posts → what are comments saying?
- ChatGPT: "What are the biggest problems someone faces as a [X] trying to [Y]?"

---

## The Most Important Principle

Bad copywriters hunt for tactics and frameworks.
Great copywriters hunt for specific conditions of their audience.

Depth of understanding of your audience **directly dictates** how effective your copy is.

If you understand your audience 5x better than your competition, you won't get 5x the results — you'll likely get 50-100x the results.

Your conditions research should be a living document. Never finished.
