# Copywriting — Text-Subtext (The Language of Emotion)
*Source: Copy Elite — Copywriting 101, Text-Subtext module*

---

## The Core Concept

Copywriting is a language. The language of emotion.

Every line of copy has two layers:
- **Text** (how) — the literal words you're writing
- **Subtext** (why) — the emotion/feeling you're trying to push

Great copy provokes emotions → emotions provoke action → action leads to sales.

**The rule:** Focus on WHY you're writing a sentence before you worry about WHAT you write. Every sentence should play a specific role in persuading the reader. No lines wasted.

---

## Text/Subtext Examples

| Text (what they read) | Subtext (what they feel) |
|---|---|
| "We've developed a step-by-step solution" | "This would be easy if I had that" |
| "What you're about to read will cause a FALLOUT" | "This is important. I need to read this." |
| "History is repeating itself" | "This seems safe/predictable. I'll know what to expect." |
| "WARNING" | "This is dangerous/important. I need to pay attention." |
| "Breakthrough Discovery" | "This is brand-new. I need to get in before people catch on." |
| "If that's you, don't worry" | "This person understands me. I can trust them." |

## The 4 General Conditions (Core Emotions)

Every piece of copy appeals to one or more of these four pre-wired human emotions. They work on everyone regardless of niche.

### 1. EASY
People have a natural bias toward simplicity and low cognitive load. Make the action you're persuading them to take appear as easy as possible.

**5 ways to push "easy":**
- **Decrease time investment:** "In just a few minutes," "starting next Monday"
- **Decrease effort investment:** "All of this by clicking a few buttons," "without (the hard work they hate)"
- **Decrease intelligence required:** "You don't have to be an expert," "simple"
- **Decrease resources required:** "With only $100," "all you need is..."
- **Decrease luck/chance required:** "Anybody," "fool-proof," "proven," "you don't need genetics"

The alter-ego of Easy = **"Anybody."** If you show anyone can succeed, people believe THEY can succeed regardless of their limiting beliefs.

**Trigger words:** Easy, Anybody, Simple, Fool-proof, Step-by-step, System, "You don't need," "All you have to do is..."

### 2. SAFE
Risk Aversion Theory — people prioritize not losing over winning. They prefer a sure, lower outcome over a gamble with higher upside but risk of loss.

Make the action appear as safe as possible. Highlight how predictable and proven the results are. Decrease perceived downside risk to zero.

The alter-ego of Safe = **"Predictable."** If you show how predictable the results are, people believe the opportunity is safe.

**Trigger words:** Proven, Predictable, "The safest way to...," "History is repeating itself...," "The last time this happened...," social proof ("85+ people just like you")

### 3. NEW
The most important emotion according to many top copywriters. People want something NEW, not an improvement on what they already know. Without seeming "new," your copy gets categorized as "something I've seen before" and they move on.

**"Categorization is DEATH."** — Kyle Milligan

Two ways to create "new":
- **New Macro-Opportunity:** reframe the entire method/vehicle (e.g. "Marketing Agency" vs "Growth Operating")
- **New Micro-Opportunity:** reframe a specific part of the process (e.g. "Cold DMs" vs "High-Value Outreach Systems")

The KEY: it must sound unique and exciting while ALSO being logical and believable. Don't be corny or weird.

The alter-ego of New = **"Only."** If it's ONLY available through your offer, it inherently feels brand-new.

**Trigger words:** NEW, Breakthrough, Discovery, One-Of-A-Kind, Disruptive, Game-Changing, Revealed

### 4. BIG / FOMO
When an opportunity seems "big," it demands attention, creates intrigue, and triggers fear of missing out. You want readers to feel something BIG is happening and they need to act FAST before they miss it.

**Trigger words:** Big, Huge, Once-In-A-Lifetime, Breakthrough, Fast, Fastest-Growing, "Only seen twice in history," World-Changing, Massive

---

## Balance

If you push these emotions too hard, you come off as salesy or dramatic. How subtly or aggressively you push depends on your audience.

**Key insight:** If your audience has been burned by direct/salesy marketing before, they'll respond negatively to that style. Calibrate to the audience.

**Rule of thumb:** Write the first draft pushing emotions to 110%, then dial it back in editing to where your audience is comfortable.

---

## Copy Review Process (Subtext Iteration)

When reviewing any piece of copy, run through these 6 questions:

1. Which of the 4 emotions is MOST important to push in this specific piece, for this specific audience?
2. Where/how can I push the **easy** emotion more?
3. Where/how can I push the **new** emotion more?
4. Where/how can I push the **safe** emotion more?
5. Where/how can I push the **big/fomo** emotion more?
6. Based on my audience and this piece, should I crank up the emotions or tone them down?
