# Copywriting — Conditions Deck Template
*Source: Copy Elite — Conditions Deck Template*
*Use this to build a Conditions Deck for any audience before writing copy.*

---

## SECTION 1 | MACRO AIM

**Audience/Niche (Who):**
- How can you easily describe the people you're targeting?
- How do they label themselves? (Helps if it 'sounds' specific but is actually broad.)

**Purpose (Why):**
- What are their main driving forces?
- What do they want at a deep level? (Maslow: safety, belonging, esteem, self-actualisation)

**Goal (What):**
- What is their big goal?
- What method are they trying to use to achieve their purpose?
- What is the problem they think will "change everything" if they solve it?

---

## SECTION 2 | THE PAINFUL DETAILS
*(Find the most powerful 3-5 for each)*

**Current Situation (Negative):**
- What is going on in their day-to-day life?
- What do they spend their time doing?
- What is happening that they feel like they can't overcome or escape?
- Think literally. Focus on what is most true for the majority of the audience.

**Dream Situation (Positive):**
- What do they want their future life to look like?
- Whose lifestyle do they envy?
- What do they want to spend their time doing?

**Pain Points (Negative — emotional):**
- How do they FEEL about the painful things in their current situation?
- What specific words trigger painful emotions for this audience?

**Desire Points (Positive — emotional):**
- How do they WANT TO FEEL about the things they desire?
- What specific words trigger deep desire in this audience?

---

## SECTION 3 | BELIEFS
*(Add as many as possible, ranked in order of importance)*

**Method False Beliefs:**
- What are all the reasons/excuses they think the method doesn't work?
- Why do they think the method sucks?

**Internal False Beliefs:**
- What are all the reasons/excuses they think they can't make the method work?
- Why do they think they personally suck?

**External False Beliefs:**
- What reasons do they have for thinking the world/environment won't allow them to succeed?
- Why do they think the external situation is against them?

**Perceived Micro-Problems:**
- In their opinion, why haven't they been able to solve the big problem yet?
- What do they consider to be the most painful small problems inside the big problem?
- What are the smaller problems they'd have to solve to get from where they are to their dream outcome?

---

## SECTION 4 | GENERAL CONDITIONS

- What general conditions (pre-wired human psychology) can you leverage?
- In what ways can you appeal to almost all humans in this copy?
- Examples: New / Easy / Safe / Big / Fast / Free / Exclusive

---

## SECTION 5 | VARIABLES OF LIKENESS
*(Relatability — how to make the reader feel like you're one of them)*

1. What words/phrases/examples/stories can you use to be more relatable to this audience?
2. How can you be more familiar and consistent with them?
3. What failures or imperfections can you share that make you more human?
4. How can you come across as warm and genuine (not corporate or salesy)?
5. How can you show competence without being arrogant? (Show, don't tell.)
6. Where can you use humor and stories?
7. How can you give genuine free value that actually helps them?
8. How can you be confident without being needy?
9. What social proof can you use without bragging?
10. Are you being non-needy? Writing like you don't desperately need them to take action?
11. **The Cult Leader 5:**
    - What **dreams** do they have — and how can you encourage them?
    - What **failures** do they have — and how can you redirect blame away from them?
    - What **fears** do they have — and how can you make them feel safe?
    - What **suspicions** do they have — and how can you confirm those suspicions?
    - What **common enemies** do you share — and how can you throw rocks at them?

---

## SECTION 6 | PROBLEM CHAIN
*(How to lead someone from "I have a vague problem" to "I need this specific thing")*

1. What are the core desires your audience is trying to fulfil?
2. What is the obvious external problem they currently blame their pain on? *(Their current awareness level — e.g. "I need to make more money")*
3. What is your unique mechanism — the specific problem your product/service actually solves? *(Must be: Specific, Unique, Causal, Simple)*
4. What is the logical chain of increasingly specific problems that takes someone from the obvious external problem → to needing your unique mechanism?
5. What sub-mechanisms fall under your unique mechanism?

**Rule:** Keep it simple. The most logical path to the conclusion. Less is more.
