# Copywriting — Pain-Confidence (The Action Equation)
*Source: Copy Elite — Copywriting 101, Pain-Confidence module*

---

## The Action Equation

Your job is to create action. Pain fuels action.

**(Pain of Inaction - Pain of Action) + Confidence in Action = Chance of Action**

Three levers to pull:
1. **Increase Pain of Inaction** (make doing nothing feel unbearable)
2. **Decrease Pain of Action** (make the action feel easy/painless)
3. **Increase Confidence in Action** (make them believe the action will actually work)

If the pain of inaction is GREATER than the pain of action, AND they're confident the action will alleviate that pain... they'll take the action.

---

## The 7 Pain Origins (Maslow-Based)

All pain traces back to one or more core desires not being met:

1. **Staying Alive** (Survival)
2. **Freedom From Fear/Danger** (Safety)
3. **Sexual Companionship** (Significant Other)
4. **Social Approval/Belonging** (Community)
5. **Status/Respect** (Superiority)
6. **Fulfilment of Purpose** (Self-Actualization)
7. **Enjoying Food & Drink** (Yummy)

Key insights:
- Most people don't realize the origin of their pain — they just feel "bad" and blame it on surface problems
- Once an origin is solved for yourself, you usually want to solve it for your family
- Your job as a copywriter: **bring awareness to the pain your audience is in** and **blame that pain on a specific problem** your product/service solves

---

## Lever 1: Increasing Pain of Inaction

Make them feel the pain of doing nothing — of things staying the same or getting worse.

You can create pain from two angles:

### Positive Angle (Running to the light)
**Dream Situation + How They Want to Feel = Pain from not being there yet**

Show them their dream future and how they want to feel. They'll subconsciously feel pain because they're NOT there yet and DON'T feel those positive emotions.

### Negative Angle (Running from the dark)
**Nightmare Situation + How They Hate Feeling = Pain from being stuck there**

Bring attention to their painful present/past/future and how bad they feel about it. They'll feel pain from being stuck.

### Examples (Positive vs Negative)
- Fitness (+): "Unlock skills that make people look at you like you're a famous athlete... in only 3-6 months"
- Fitness (-): "Say goodbye to the embarrassment of looking, feeling, and BEING frail... in only 3-6 months"
- YouTube Title (+): "The 5 Habits Of The TOP 1% Men"
- YouTube Title (-): "The 5 Habits Keeping MEN Weak"

### Storyselling (Bonus Technique)
Tell the story of going from a painful current situation to a painless dream situation. The audience imagines themselves in your shoes and indirectly becomes aware of their own pain. Especially powerful when the product/service is tied to a personal brand.

### Critical Reminder
The amount of pain you create depends on how well you understand and use specific conditions, general conditions, and the variables of likeness. If you talk about situations that DON'T match what your audience feels, you won't create any pain. Specific conditions are everything.

You can also increase pain of inaction by pushing Big/FOMO and using urgency/scarcity.

---

## Lever 2: Decreasing Pain of Action

Make the action you want them to take seem as easy, simple, fast, and painless as possible.

This is done by pushing the general conditions from the Text-Subtext module — New, Easy, Safe emotions.

**Make it easy for them to take the action you want.**

---

## Lever 3: Creating Confidence

Once you've increased pain and decreased friction, you need to make them believe the action will actually work. Three phases:

### Phase 1: Confidence in You/Your Client
Build trust using the 11 Variables of Likeness (from Liking-Relatability module) plus specificity. This is done throughout the entire funnel but especially at the top.

### Phase 2: Confidence in Themselves
They need to believe THEY can succeed with this, not just that it works for others. This is vital when selling something that requires effort on their part.

**How to do it:** Write down every false belief (reason/excuse) they might have about not being able to succeed. Then disprove each one using logical arguments, case studies, testimonials, personal stories.

### Phase 3: Confidence in the Method & Your Solution
Make them believe the method/opportunity AND your specific product/service are the right ones.

Tools for building confidence in the method:
- Push the "New/Only" emotion
- Specificity in claims and results
- Testimonials and results
- General excellence/professionalism
- Clear logical argument (Problem Chain)
- Proof and logic: case studies, demonstrations, testimonials, authority quotes, visuals, official studies, risk-reversal (guarantees), believability of claims
