# Copywriting Frameworks

> The copy on the site does as much work as the design. Use these formulas to write each section. Pull from the student's interview answers, but rewrite for impact. Don't paste their raw answers — refine.

---

## Voice rules

**Tone:** Confident, direct, opinionated. The student is positioned as an operator, not a guru. They get results. They don't waffle.

**Person:** First person ("I", "my", "me"). Never "we" — it's a personal site, not a team page.

**Banned phrases:**
- "World-class" / "industry-leading" / "cutting-edge"
- "Synergy", "leverage" (as a verb), "ecosystem"
- "Imagine if…" / "What if I told you…"
- "In today's fast-paced world…"
- "Here's the thing…" / "The truth is…"
- Any cliché that sounds AI-generated

**Banned punctuation:**
- **Em dashes (—)** — the kit author does NOT use em dashes. Use periods, commas, or rewrite. Hyphens are fine.
- Excessive exclamation points (max 1 per page, only if absolutely needed)

**Sentence rhythm:** Mix short and long. Short sentences punch. Long sentences explain. A wall of medium sentences is the AI signature.

Example of good rhythm:
> "I help fitness coaches scale. Specifically, those stuck at £3-£10k/mo who want to break six figures without burning out. AI does the heavy lifting. You stay in your zone of genius."

## Hero headline

The single most important piece of copy on the page. It must:
1. State who they help
2. State the outcome
3. Mention AI (this is the AI Partner Method — the differentiator must be AI)
4. Be one sentence

**Templates (pick the best fit for the student):**

### A. The straight claim
> I help [audience] [outcome] using AI.

Example: "I help fitness coaches scale to £10k/mo using AI systems."

### B. The contrarian
> [Audience] don't need [common solution]. They need [your solution].

Example: "Coaches don't need more content. They need AI that books calls while they sleep."

### C. The specific result
> From [bad state] to [good state] in [timeframe]. Using AI.

Example: "From 0 to 47 booked calls a week in 30 days. Using AI."

### D. The accent underline (visually elegant)
> [Statement of work] for [audience]. <span class="accent-underline">[Promise / outcome].</span>

Example: "Performance-only growth for coaches. Pay only when it works."

**Default to A** unless the student's positioning suggests otherwise.

---

## Hero sub-paragraph

One or two sentences directly under the headline. Expands the promise.

**Formula:** [Specific mechanism]. [Outcome restated more concretely].

Example: "AI systems that handle DM outreach, lead qualification, and call booking. So you spend your time closing, not chasing."

---

## Stats labels

The big number is the visual hook. The label tells the story.

**Format:** Past-tense or noun phrase. Short.

Good:
- "£250k generated for clients"
- "47 businesses scaled"
- "23x average ROI"

Bad:
- "Some money I made for people" (vague)
- "We have helped over forty-seven companies grow!" (wordy + "we")
- "Hundreds of happy customers" (unspecific number)

If the student has no numbers yet, use credibility instead:
- "5+ years in [their industry]"
- "AI Partner Method certified"
- "Built [N] AI systems"

## About section

Three short paragraphs max. Each does a specific job:

**Paragraph 1 — The hook:** What's true now. One sentence.
> "I'm a 27-year-old AI Growth Operator from Manchester."

**Paragraph 2 — The before:** What they did before. Two-three sentences. Vulnerable but not whiny.
> "I spent 5 years grinding agency life. £4k/mo, 70-hour weeks, no time to live. I knew I was good at this. I just couldn't scale myself."

**Paragraph 3 — The shift:** What changed. Two sentences. End on the offer.
> "Then I learned the AI Partner Method. Now I run lean, deliver results, and only work with clients who deserve my time."

**Headline above this:** Short, punchy, opinionated.

Examples:
- "I'm not a guru. I'm an operator."
- "Years grinding. Then AI changed everything."
- "I built this so you don't have to."

## Services / What I Do

Each service card has a name and 1-2 sentence description.

**Card name:** Plain English, not jargon.

Good:
- "AI lead generation"
- "Automated DM systems"
- "Done-for-you content engine"

Bad:
- "Synergistic AI Growth Solutions" (jargon)
- "The Method" (vague)
- "AI Consulting" (boring)

**Card description formula:** [What it does]. [What it gets you].

Example: "I build AI-powered DM systems that qualify leads in your sleep. You wake up to booked calls, not unread messages."

---

## Process steps

Three short labels. One-line description each.

**Format:** Verb-noun.

Good:
- "Audit your funnel" / "We map every leak in your client flow."
- "Build your systems" / "I deploy custom AI tools tailored to your offer."
- "Scale your output" / "We compound. More leads, more closes, less work."

Bad:
- "Discovery" (too consultanty)
- "Implementation" (too consultanty)
- "Optimization" (too consultanty)

If the student gave consultant-y words, rewrite into operator-y verbs.

---

## Who it's for

Two columns: yes and no. Be specific. Specific qualifications make people self-select.

**"This is for you if" examples:**
- "You run a coaching/agency/info offer doing £3-£20k/mo"
- "You're stuck at the same revenue for 6+ months"
- "You're already converting — you just need more volume"
- "You'd rather hire a system than another VA"

**"This isn't for you if" examples:**
- "You're starting from zero with no offer"
- "You expect overnight results"
- "You want to be hands-off — this needs your input"
- "You're not willing to invest in tools that print money"

The "not for you" column is more important than the "for you" column. It's where qualification really happens.

## Testimonial structure (placeholders)

Each placeholder has a quote, name, role. Quote should be 2-3 sentences and feel real but be obviously labeled placeholder.

**Format:**
> "[2-3 sentence specific outcome statement.]"
> — [Client Name], [Role / Business]

Three placeholders cover three angles:
1. Speed of result
2. Specific revenue/lead outcome
3. Quality-of-life / freedom outcome

## FAQ answers

Each answer: 2-4 sentences. Don't be defensive. Don't oversell. Just answer.

**Tone reference:**
> Q: How long does it take to see results?
> A: First leads usually come in week 2. Real revenue from those leads, week 4-6. I won't promise overnight — anyone who does is lying.

> Q: What if I'm not technical?
> A: That's the point. I build the systems. You run your business. If you can use Instagram, you can use what I set up.

> Q: How is this different from a course?
> A: Courses give you information. I give you working systems plus the guidance to keep them working. You're paying for outcome, not lessons.

Be honest. Be direct. Don't dodge.

## Final CTA

The last push. One headline, one supporting line, one button.

**Headline templates:**
- "Ready to grow?"
- "Let's build it."
- "Stop guessing. Start operating."
- "If you're still here, you know."

**Supporting line:**
> "Book a free call. We'll talk through your bottleneck and map a path forward."

or

> "30 minutes. No pitch. We figure out if this is a fit."

**Button:** Same as nav and hero — "Book a call ↗"

---

## Things to ALWAYS rewrite from the student's interview

Students will give you raw answers. Almost none of them are publishable as-is. Always rewrite:

- **"I help anyone who wants to grow"** → pick a specific audience, even if you have to nudge them
- **"I'm passionate about helping people"** → cut. Replace with something concrete.
- **"I do AI consulting"** → rewrite as outcome ("I help X get Y")
- **Long rambling about their journey** → distill to 3 sentences
- **"We"** → switch to "I"

The student trusts you to make their copy good. Don't paste their words verbatim if better words exist.
