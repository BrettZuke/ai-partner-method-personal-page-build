## Module 1 — Specific Conditions

### The Lock & Key Principle
Copy only works when it matches the SPECIFIC CONDITIONS of the person reading it.
- General copy = general results. Specific copy = specific (powerful) results.
- Think of your audience's mind as a lock. Your copy is the key. Only the exact right key opens it.

### General vs Specific Conditions
**General Conditions** = emotional states everyone wants (Easy, Safe, New, Big/FOMO) — the subtext layer.
**Specific Conditions** = the exact circumstances of your specific audience RIGHT NOW — their beliefs, fears, language, awareness, desires.

### The Conditions Deck
Before writing anything, map out:
1. **Who are they?** (demographics, job, life situation)
2. **Where are they right now?** (what problem are they stuck on, what have they tried)
3. **What do they want?** (dream outcome, core desire)
4. **What do they believe?** (about the problem, about solutions, about themselves)
5. **What's their awareness level?** (do they know the problem exists? do they know your solution?)
6. **What language do they use?** (exact words they say when describing their problem)

### How to Find Specific Conditions
- Sales calls / DMs — listen to how they describe their pain in their own words
- Personal experience — if you've been where they are, mine that memory
- Comments, forums, subreddits — where do they vent? what words do they use?
- Surveys / market research (see: `dan_bennett_market_research.md` for live example)

## Module 2 — Text-Subtext

### The Core Distinction
- **Text** = the HOW — the surface-level mechanism, the words, the specific angle
- **Subtext** = the WHY — the emotion underneath. What they actually feel. What they secretly want.

Copy fails when it only operates at the Text level. Great copy works at the Subtext level and uses Text as the delivery mechanism.

### The 4 General Conditions (Subtexts)
These are the 4 emotional states that make people take action. Every piece of copy should lean into at least one.

1. **Easy** — "I can do this without it being painful/difficult." Remove friction. Show it's simpler than they think.
2. **Safe** — "I won't get hurt/scammed/embarrassed." Reduce risk. Build trust. Show proof.
3. **New** — "This is something I haven't tried/seen before." This is the most powerful — and most misunderstood.
   - **CATEGORIZATION IS DEATH.** If your audience can put your offer in a box they've seen before ("oh, it's just another course"), they'll apply all their previous bad experiences to it.
   - New doesn't mean gimmicky. It means presenting your solution as a fresh mechanism they haven't encountered.
4. **Big/FOMO** — "This is massive. If I miss it, I'll regret it." Scale of opportunity. Wealth transfer. Once-in-a-generation window.

### The Price Is Right Framework (applying it)
1. Find their Specific Conditions (who they are, where they are, what they believe)
2. Choose which Subtext/General Condition is most compelling for THIS audience RIGHT NOW
3. Choose the Text (angle, mechanism, framing) that best delivers that subtext

## Module 3 — Liking-Relatability

### The Bridge of Trust
People don't buy from people they don't trust. Trust is built through perceived SIMILARITY.
The more your audience feels "this person is like me" or "this person understands me," the more they trust — and the more they buy.

### 11 Variables of Likeness
Use these to create connection and trust in your copy:

1. **Shared Values** — "I believe what you believe."
2. **Shared Identity** — "I am who you are." (background, tribe, lifestyle)
3. **Shared Pain** — "I've felt what you've felt." (origin story, relatability)
4. **Shared Enemy** — "We hate the same thing." (guru culture, 9-5 prisons, scams)
5. **Shared Language** — "I talk how you talk." (slang, idioms, cultural references)
6. **Shared Goals** — "I want what you want."
7. **Shared Humor** — "I laugh at what you laugh at."
8. **Shared Beliefs About the World** — "I see the world how you see it."
9. **Familiarity** — "I've seen you before. You feel known."
10. **Admiration/Aspiration** — "You've done what I want to do." (proof, status)
11. **Physical Similarity** — "You look like me." (relevant in video/image content)

### The Cult Leader 5
The 5 deepest trust triggers. If you nail all 5, they'd follow you anywhere:
1. **You understand me better than anyone else.** (specific pain articulation)
2. **You are like me / I want to be like you.** (identity bridge)
3. **You have what I want.** (proof, results, lifestyle)
4. **You can get me what I want.** (credibility + mechanism)
5. **You care about me, not just my money.** (authenticity, no bullshit, pushes them away to pull them in)

## Module 4 — Pain-Confidence

### The Action Equation
**Pain of Inaction − Pain of Action + Confidence = Chance of Taking Action**

- **Pain of Inaction** = how much does it hurt to stay where they are? (raise this)
- **Pain of Action** = how much does it cost/hurt to take action? (lower this — price, effort, risk, time)
- **Confidence** = how sure are they that this will work for THEM? (increase this)

The goal: maximize the equation. Make staying the same unbearable. Make acting feel easy, safe, and inevitable.

### 7 Origins of Pain
Where pain comes from — use these to diagnose and amplify:
1. **Financial** — broke, stuck, not earning enough
2. **Physical** — health, body, energy, appearance
3. **Social/Status** — invisible, disrespected, left behind
4. **Romantic/Sexual** — lonely, can't attract who they want
5. **Purpose/Meaning** — feels pointless, no direction
6. **Freedom/Autonomy** — trapped, no control, owned by others
7. **Identity** — "I'm not who I want to be. I don't trust myself."

### Positive vs Negative Angle
- **Positive (Dream Outcome)**: Paint the life they want — freedom, status, income, lifestyle. "Imagine waking up..."
- **Negative (Pain Amplification)**: Rub the wound. "Every day you don't act, another lad gets the spot you could have had."
- Best copy uses both: establish the dream, remind them of the nightmare, show the bridge.

### 3 Phases of Creating Confidence
1. **Proof** — show results. Testimonials, case studies, numbers, real examples. Make it believable and specific.
2. **Mechanism Believability** — is the HOW logical and simple enough? If they can't follow the logic, they won't believe it works.
3. **Self-Efficacy** — do they believe THEY specifically can do it? Destroy "that works for other people, not me." ("If I could do it from a caravan delivering pizzas, you can do it.")

## Module 5 — Problem Chains

### Value Creation Formula
**PAIN → PROBLEM → SOLUTION → EFFECTIVENESS = VALUE CREATED ($)**

- If they're not in pain, they won't look for a problem.
- If they don't connect their pain to YOUR problem, they'll never find your solution.
- If they don't believe yours is the best/only solution, they won't choose it.
- If they don't believe they have to decide NOW, they'll procrastinate.

### Key Breakthrough: Sell the Audience on the Problem BEFORE the Solution
"Build it and they will come" only works if you have something truly extraordinary. In the meantime: SELL the problem first. The sales process IS the first step to creating value.

### Pain Buckets
Your job as a copywriter: create pain → attach it to a SPECIFIC problem → create desire to solve that problem.

Problems are 'pain buckets' — containers that pain gets poured into. The more pain you attach to a specific problem (the one YOUR solution solves), the more pressure to act.

Example structure: "Can't get X? It's because of [your unique mechanism/problem]. If you had [your solution], you could."

### Unique Mechanisms
A unique mechanism = a specific, intriguing name for the specific problem your solution solves.

Effective unique mechanisms are: **Specific. Unique. Casual. Simple.**

Examples:
- Kinobody: 'Intermittent Fasting'
- Iman: 'SMMA'
- Clickfunnels: 'Funnels'
- P90x: 'Muscle Confusion'

WARNING: What's unique changes over time. SMMA was fresh in 2020. By 2025 it's tired. New mechanisms emerged: 'Growth Operating', 'IPGA', 'AI Partner Method'.

### Sub-Mechanisms
Sub-mechanisms = unique mechanisms WITHIN your main mechanism that solve the micro-problems involved in achieving results.

They exist to **destroy limiting beliefs** around your mechanism. When someone says "yeah but what about X problem?" — the sub-mechanism answers that.

To create sub-mechanisms:
1. Break result into 3-5 steps
2. Identify biggest false beliefs around each step
3. Create a casual, specific name for the solution to each

### The Problem Chain (Full Structure)
Map this out for every offer:
```
CORE DESIRE(S)
  ↓ (internal problem, often unaware — origin of pain)
THE BIG PROBLEM / GOAL
  ↓ (obvious external problem they currently blame for pain)
PROBLEM FRACTALS
  ↓ (increasingly specific sub-problems they believe they need to solve)
UNIQUE MECHANISM
  ↓ (the specific problem/opportunity your product solves)
SUB-MECHANISMS
  (solutions to micro-problems inside the unique mechanism)
```

The goal: prove your unique mechanism is the FINAL DOMINO that leads to solving all their bigger problems. If you do that, they'll have an overwhelming urge to buy.

## Module 6 — Urgency-Scarcity

### Why It Matters: Cognitive Ease & Procrastination
The brain is a lazy optimizer. It avoids hard decisions by procrastinating ("I'll do it later").
Procrastination wins because: (1) you don't suffer the pain of doing now, (2) you trick yourself into thinking you'll still do it later.

Urgency and scarcity force a decision NOW. **We want a 'yes' or 'no' — never 'maybe.'**

Both stem from **loss aversion** — humans fear losing more than they desire gaining.

### Forms of Urgency (limited time)
1. **Rolling Cohorts** — next cohort starts X date, miss it and wait
2. **Seasonal Urgency** — only available this time of year
3. **Pricing Urgency** — price increases after deadline
4. **Exploding Opportunity** — rare external window (gold rush vibes — act before it closes)
5. **Bonus Urgency** — extra bonuses only for fast movers
6. **Event-Based Urgency** — registration closes when we go live
7. **Countdown Timers** — visual ticking clock
8. **Fast-Action Urgency** — bonus only for those who act within X minutes
9. **External Deadline** — platform/tool access expiring
10. **Personal Deadline (Self-Imposed)** — "I can hold your spot until tomorrow"

### Forms of Scarcity (limited quantity)
1. **Limited Supply** — set number of spots/units, once gone, gone
2. **Limited Bonuses** — special bonuses only for fastest movers
3. **Never Available Again** — this version of offer won't return
4. **Dripped Availability** — only X new slots per month
5. **Tiered Scarcity** — different bonuses for first X, next Y buyers
6. **Scarcity by Qualification** — must apply/be approved
7. **Time + Quantity** — deadline AND limited spots, whichever comes first

### Urgency + Emotional Leverage (most powerful combo)
Pair urgency with a dream outcome or pain point for maximum impact:
- Weak: "Enroll before Friday to get $100 off"
- Strong: "If you're serious about not being told when to wake up, where to be, or how much you're worth — this is your chance. Enrollment closes Friday. After that, it's back to being owned by someone else's schedule."

Top/Bottom duality: contrast their dream state (top of the mountain) with their current state (rock bottom) while applying urgency.

### Keys to Effective Urgency
1. It must be REAL — people smell fake deadlines
2. Tie to real events (launches, price changes, cohort starts)
3. Remind frequently — most people delay until the last second
4. Give a logical reason WHY ("we start calls Monday, can't let people in late")

### Keys to Effective Scarcity
1. Be specific with numbers ("3 VIP slots left" > "limited spots")
2. Make it visible — live counter, struck-through bonuses
3. Follow through — if you cap it, actually cap it
4. Explain why it's limited (real reason, not "to make you buy")

## Module 7 — Concise-Curiosity

### The C.U.B. Method
Robert Bly's framework. The 3 reasons a reader stops reading:
- **C** — Confusing Copy
- **U** — Unbelievable Copy
- **B** — Boring Copy

The process:
1. Write emotional rough drafts (volume, no judgment — just spew)
2. Break → come back → fix anything CONFUSING
3. Break → come back → fix anything UNBELIEVABLE
4. Break → come back → fix anything BORING

### Fixing Confusing Copy
- Remove esoteric points — people care about results, not how
- Avoid techno-babble — 3rd-5th grade reading level
- Vague = bad — use specific, concrete, VISUAL language
- ONE big idea per piece — don't go down rabbit holes

### Fixing Unbelievable Copy
- Cut the claim or make it more believable
- Add very specific and compelling proof
- Relate to the reader using specific conditions
- Claims must be exciting BUT realistic

### Fixing Boring Copy
- Don't talk about what they already know — match their awareness level
- Push emotions harder (Text-Subtext, Pain-Confidence)
- Vary sentence length — Mountain Copy not Brick Copy
- Concrete, visual language — they should have a movie playing in their head
- Simplify and condense — every word earns its keep

### Curiosity: The Knowledge Gap
Curiosity = you've opened a gap between what they know and what they need to know to get their desired outcome. They HAVE to scratch the itch.

Your job: **grab attention by creating the gap — hold attention by NOT closing it too soon.**

Biggest beginner mistake: giving away too much too fast. String them along.

### 16 Ways to Create Curiosity
1. **Glipse of Solution** — "Most people are one sentence away from doubling their prices."
2. **Provocative Question** — "What if everything you knew about productivity was wrong?"
3. **Contradiction/Paradox** — "We stopped posting content... and grew faster."
4. **Story Loop (Cliffhanger)** — "I was 2 days away from quitting... and then this happened."
5. **Imply Hidden Secret** — "Nobody talks about this part of freelancing…"
6. **Shock/Pattern Interrupt** — "We deleted 30% of our list… and made more money."
7. **Tease the Result, Not the How** — "He went from $0 to $10k/month in 43 days."
8. **Odd or Specific Details** — "The $7 PDF that made us $100,000."
9. **Turn the Mirror on the Reader** — "Are you unknowingly pushing leads away?"
10. **Call Out Mistakes/Hidden Problems** — "Most businesses sabotage their sales calls in the first 10 seconds."
11. **Incomplete Comparisons** — "This sales funnel works like Netflix, but better."
12. **Taboo/'Forbidden' Framing** — "The persuasion trick banned from TV ads."
13. **Numbers Without Context** — "$22,000 in one day — and it wasn't even a launch."
14. **Visual Curiosity Triggers** — Blurred faces, out-of-context screenshots, weird expressions
15. **Time Bomb Framing** — "We only have 4 days to make this work."
16. **Curiosity Power Words** — "Weird trick", "Nobody talks about this", "Insider secret", "Stupidly simple", "Almost gave up", "The internet lied to you"

### Holding Attention
Two variables:
1. How compelling your hook/knowledge gap is (how deep did you dig the hook?)
2. The pace at which you release information (drip-feed — give just enough to feel progress, not enough to feel satisfied)

Copy structure should feel like a murder mystery: open a juicy loop → drop intriguing clues → unexpected turns → epic pay-off.

## Module 8 — Synthesis

### The Synthesis Checklist (use for every piece of copy)

**Phase 1 — Foundations (before writing)**
1. What are the specific conditions of who I'm writing to?
2. What is the micro-action goal of THIS piece? (click CTA, open email, etc.)
3. What is the macro-action goal of the system this piece sits in? (build trust, sell the program, etc.)
4. What are the rules of the environment? (email vs IG reel vs webinar vs ad)

**Phase 2 — Rough Drafts**
1. What is the ONE big idea? (summarize entire sales message in one sentence)
2. What is the main emotion? (Easy / Safe / New / Big — which is most compelling for THIS audience?)
3. What sections are needed and in what order?
4. Write as much volume as possible without judgment — DON'T THINK, FEEL
5. Multiple drafts until you FEEL the emotion come across

**Phase 3 — Refined Draft**
1. How can emotions be MORE potent? (benefits not features, vivid/specific/painful language)
2. How can you build trust using the 11 Variables of Likeness?
3. Does the text match the voice? (authentic to the person/brand)
4. Is the hook/lead/angle as curious as possible? (knowledge gap, right question implied)
5. Have you added urgency/scarcity?

**Phase 4 — Final Draft**
1. Remove & fix anything CONFUSING (C.U.B. method)
2. Remove & fix anything UNBELIEVABLE
3. Remove & fix anything BORING
4. SIMPLIFY & CONDENSE
5. Wait 24 hours — come back fresh
6. Repeat Phase 4 multiple times for high-value pieces
